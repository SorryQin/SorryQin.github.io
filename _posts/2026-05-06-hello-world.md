I've been meaning to start a blog for a while. As a graduate student working in AI and computer vision, I spend most of my days reading papers, running experiments, and debugging models. Writing things down — in a way that's clear and shareable — feels like the missing piece.

## Why now?

There are a few reasons:

1. **Clarify my thinking.** Explaining a concept forces you to understand it deeply. Every time I write about a paper or technique, I discover gaps in my own understanding.

2. **Build a public record.** Research moves fast. Having a place to document what I'm learning — papers I've read, techniques I've tried, problems I've solved — creates a valuable reference for myself and others.

3. **Share the journey.** Graduate school is challenging. The path from coursework to research to industry isn't always straightforward. I hope my experiences can help others navigating similar paths.

## What to expect

This blog will cover:

- **Paper notes and summaries** — deep dives into interesting CV/ML papers I'm reading
- **Technical tutorials** — practical guides on implementing models, debugging training pipelines, and working with vision datasets
- **Research reflections** — thoughts on the research process, experiment design, and lessons learned
- **Career updates** — milestones, internships, and the transition from academia to industry

## A bit about the stack

This site is built with Jekyll and hosted on GitHub Pages. Blog posts are written in Markdown, version-controlled with Git, and deployed automatically on push. Simple, fast, and free.
