Hello World — Starting a Technical Blog

I've been meaning to start a blog for a while. As a graduate student working in AI and computer vision, I spend most of my days reading papers, running experiments, and debugging models. Writing things down — in a way that's clear and shareable — feels like the missing piece.

Why now?

There are a few reasons:

  1. Clarify my thinking. Explaining a concept forces you to understand it deeply. Every time I write about a paper or technique, I discover gaps in my own understanding.
  2. Build a public record. Research moves fast. Having a place to document what I'm learning — papers I've read, techniques I've tried, problems I've solved — creates a valuable reference for myself and others.
  3. Share the journey. Graduate school is challenging. I hope my experiences can help others navigating similar paths.

What to expect

This blog will cover:

  • Paper notes and summaries — deep dives into interesting CV/ML papers
  • Technical tutorials — practical guides on implementing models and working with vision datasets
  • Research reflections — thoughts on the research process and lessons learned
  • Career updates — milestones and the transition from academia to industry

A bit about the stack

This site is built with plain HTML/CSS/JS and hosted on GitHub Pages. Blog posts are written as HTML files, version-controlled with Git, and deployed automatically on push. Simple, fast, and free.

If you're reading this — thanks for stopping by. More to come soon.
